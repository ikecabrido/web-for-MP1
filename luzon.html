About Luzon

Luzon is the largest and most economically important island of the Philippines. It is home to more than half of the Filipino population and the site of Manila, the nation's capital and major metropolis. Luzon is part of the Philippine Mobile Belt, a fast deforming plate boundary zone between two opposing subduction zones. Luzon has many tourist attractions, such as beaches, volcanoes, historical sites, and natural parks.

Places to visit in Luzon

Underground River

Puerto Princesa Underground River is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Palawan, Philippines. It is one of the longest navigable subterranean rivers in the world. It features stunning rock formations, diverse wildlife, and a cathedral-like cave. A must-see natural wonder

Location: Sitio Sabang, Bgy. Cabayugan, Puerto Princesa City

Mt. Mayon

Mayon Volcano is an active stratovolcano in Albay, Philippines. It is known for its perfect cone shape and symmetrical slopes. It offers scenic views, hiking trails, and cultural heritage. It is one of the most iconic landmarks and tourist attractions in the country

Location: Province of Albay

Learn more
Banaue Rice Terraces

Sanaue Rice Terraces are ancient terraces carved into the mountains of Ifugao by the Igorot people. They are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a marvel of engineering and culture. They offer stunning views, cultural immersion, and eco-tourism

Location: In the mountains of Ifugao

Learn more
Pagsanjan Falls

Pagsanjan Falls is a stunning waterfall in Laguna, Philippines. It is famous for its thrilling boat ride, known as shooting the rapids, that takes visitors through the gorge and to the falls. It is also a historical and cultural site, as it was featured in the movie Apocalypse Now.

Location: Laguna Philippines

Learn more
Historic City of Vigan

The historic city of Vigan is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Philippines. It showcases the fusion of Philippine, Chinese, and European cultures in its architecture, cuisine, and crafts. It is famous for its cobblestone streets, ancestral houses, churches, museums, and pottery

Location: Vigan, Ilocos Sur

Learn more
Kayangan Lake

Kayangan Lake is a crystal-clear freshwater lake in Coron, Palawan. It is known as the cleanest lake in Asia and offers stunning views of the surrounding limestone cliffs and underwater rock formations. It is a popular spot for swimming, snorkeling, and photography.

Location: Coron, Palawan

Learn more
Big Lagoon

Big Lagoon is a breathtaking natural wonder in El Nido, Palawan. It is a large body of turquoise water surrounded by towering limestone cliffs and lush vegetation. It is a perfect place for kayaking, snorkeling, or simply admiring the scenery

Location: El Nido, Palawan

Learn more
Honda Bay

Honda Bay is a beautiful bay in Palawan, famous for its island-hopping tours. It has white sand beaches, clear blue waters, and colorful coral reefs. It is a great place to enjoy snorkeling, swimming, and relaxing in nature

Location: Palawan

Learn more
Sabang Beach

Sabang Beach is a popular beach in Puerto Galera, known for its scuba diving sites and proximity to the Underground River. It has fine sand, clear water, and scenic views. It is a great place to enjoy nature, adventure, and relaxation

Location: Puerto Galera
