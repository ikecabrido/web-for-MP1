About Visayas

Visayas is a group of islands in the central part of the Philippines, where you can discover a rich and diverse culture and history, as well as stunning natural attractions. Visayas is home to the oldest city in the country, Cebu, where you can visit the historic Magellan's Cross, the Basilica del Santo Ni√±o, and the Temple of Leah. Visayas is also where you can find the world-famous Boracay Island, with its white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and lively nightlife. Visayas is also a haven for ecotourism, where you can explore the Chocolate Hills of Bohol, the whale sharks of Oslob, the coral reefs of Apo Island, and the tarsiers of Corella. Visayas is a destination that will make you appreciate the beauty and diversity of the Philippines.

Places to visit in Visayas

Tumalog Falls

Tumalog Falls is a single-tier curtain waterfall in Oslob, Cebu. It is about a hundred meters high, with cold water drizzling on a mossy wall. It has a shallow yet large pool and a magical atmosphere. It is a stunning sight to behold

Location: Oslob, Cebu

Learn more
Simala Church

Simala Church is a Roman Catholic pilgrimage church dedicated to the Virgin Mary in Sibonga, Cebu. It is also known as the miracle healing church because of the miraculous events that happened there. It has a castle-like structure and a serene atmosphere

Location: Sibonga, Cebu

Learn more
Moalboal Sardine Run

Moalboal is a town in Cebu, Philippines, where tourists can swim with millions of sardines. The sardine run is a natural and ethical underwater spectacle that can be reached from Panagsama Beach.

Location: Moalboal, Cebu

Learn more
Mt. Caningag

Mt. Caningag is a hilltop resort and park in Pintuyan, Southern Leyte, Philippines. It offers a breathtaking view of the mountains and islands, such as Dinagat Islands, Limasa Island, and Surigao Strait. It is known as the Little Batanes of Southern Leyte because of its scenic landscape

Location: Pintuyan, Southern Leyte

Learn more
Kuting Reef Resort

Kuting Reef Resort is a beach resort in Macrohon, Southern Leyte, Philippines. It has a private beach, a pool, and a spa. It is close to the historic island of Limasawa and the Busay Falls. It is a popular destination for diving, snorkeling, and relaxing

Location: Macrohon, Southern Leyte

Learn more
Mahayahay Falls

Mahayahay Falls, also known as Karap-agan Falls, is a two-tier waterfall in Matalom, Leyte, Philippines. It has thin waters that cascade over the rocks, creating a soothing sound. It is surrounded by green trees and vegetation, making it a serene and scenic spot.

Location: Matalom, Leyte

Learn more
Boracay Beach

Boracay Explore Boracay Beach is a famous white-sand beach in Malay, Aklan, Philippines. It is a popular destination for water activities, island hopping, and nightlife. It has many resorts, restaurants, and shops along its 4-kilometer stretch. It is also home to the endangered flying foxes

Location: Malay, Aklan

Learn more
Chocolate Hills

Chocolate Hills Explore Chocolate Hills are a natural wonder in Bohol, Philippines. They are over 1,000 hills that look like chocolate mounds when the grass turns brown. They are a National Geological Monument and a famous tourist attraction. They have an adventure park with a bicycle zipline

Location: Bohol

Learn more
Mambukal Mountain Resort

Mambukal Mountain Resort is a relaxing getaway in Murcia, Negros Occidental, Philippines. It is near Mount Kanlaon and has cool temperatures. It offers hot springs, waterfalls, boating, camping, and spa services. It is also a habitat for fruit bats and butterflies

Location: Murcia, Negros Occidental
